Yummly Recipe Auto-Conversion

This project served as my internship assignment at Whirlpool Corporation. Within Whirlpool's Yummly brand, a substantial recipe catalog awaited transformation into a structured JSON guided recipe format. The existing process involved manual translation and integration of temperature data, leading to inefficiencies. My mission? Develop an automated tool for seamless conversion of Yummly recipes from word document to JSON while seamlessly integrating temperature data. This innovation streamlines the testing process, enabling the food science team to promptly validate recipes for the guided recipe scheme. Additionally, I had the privilege to propose conceptual design enhancements, fostering an even more streamlined workflow.

On this project, I leveraged a diverse tech stack, including Python, Dart/Flutter, JSON, AWS caching, and generative AI (text-davinci-003 model). My role also demanded proficiency in working with various APIs. Beyond technical skills, I applied my expertise in data analysis and presentation, critical thinking, and problem-solving. Collaborating with a globally dispersed team spanning Italy, China, and the USA enriched my experience, fostering effective cross-cultural teamwork.

Unmasking Risk on Midwest Roads

I collaborated with two fellow students in my Computational Statistics class on this project, where our objective was to identify the primary road conditions influencing car accident severity in the Midwest. Our analysis utilized the real-time US Accidents dataset spanning 2016-2023, gathered from various traffic APIs. We primarily employed the R programming language for this project.

We employed SEM (Structural Equation Modeling) with six factors, and our results indicated that Time of Day, Driver Sight, Thunderstorm, Junction, and Humidity were statistically significant contributors to accident severity in the Midwest. Notably, Time of Day (morning, evening) exhibited the most substantial impact on accident severity. Upon deeper investigation, we found that Temperature primarily drove the Time of Day variable. This underscores the significant influence of temperature variations throughout the day on accident severity.

We recommended the implementation of targeted interventions during high-risk time periods as a potential strategy to mitigate accident severity. These interventions might include measures to improve visibility, stricter enforcement of traffic regulations, and public awareness campaigns, particularly during peak periods like the evening rush hour.

In The Art

I worked on this project with three other women, all Computer Science majors from colleges across the US, for Rewriting the Code's 48-hour Black Wings Hacks hackathon in February 2023. In the Art is a website for an art community, meant to connect creatives in the art space (painters, sculptors, musicians, dancers etc) from underrepresented communities through community-centered events. We created In the Art because of the lack of easily accessible art education/programs in these communities.

We created the website using one of Major League Hacking's developer tools, Velo by Wix. which saved us time as we didn't have to build the website from scratch. We also used MongoDB, Python, Postman APIs.

This project won the Honorable Mention prize at this hackathon, which as first-time hackers was quite an accomplishment for us. We were also particularly proud of creating something that gives back to our communities.
